# Retail SKU Analysis — Reliance Mart

**Pareto (80/20) Analysis on 500+ SKUs | Power BI + Excel | Jan 2026**

---

## Business Problem

Reliance Mart store management needed clarity on which products were actually
driving revenue — and which were occupying shelf space and inventory capital
with minimal return.

**Core question:** Which 20% of SKUs contribute 80% of total store revenue?

---

## My Role

Conducted as a live business analytics project in a team of 3 as part of the
PGDM programme at I Business Institute, Noida.

---

## Dataset

- Scope: 500+ active SKUs across product categories
- Key fields: SKU ID, Product Category, Units Sold, Revenue, Month
- Note: Data is proprietary to the retail partner and not uploaded here

---

## Tools Used

- **Power BI** — Dashboard creation and KPI visualisation
- **Microsoft Excel** — Data cleaning, Pareto calculation, pivot analysis

---

## Approach

1. **Data Cleaning** — Standardised category names, removed duplicates,
   handled missing revenue entries
2. **Pareto Ranking** — Sorted all 500+ SKUs by cumulative revenue contribution
3. **80/20 Identification** — Flagged the SKU tier crossing the 78% revenue threshold
4. **Dashboard Build** — Built an interactive Power BI report with category
   and time-period filters for store management

---

## Key Findings

- Top 20% of SKUs contributed **78% of total store revenue**
- Bottom 30% of SKUs contributed less than 4% of revenue
- 3 product categories consistently dominated revenue across all months
- Clear seasonal spikes identified in specific SKU clusters

---

## Business Recommendation

> Focus promotional spend, shelf visibility, and restocking priority on
> the top-performing SKU tier. Review and phase out the bottom 15% of SKUs
> showing no seasonal recovery pattern.

Recommendations were **adopted by store management** and incorporated into
the next quarter's restocking plan.

---

## Outcome

- Delivered structured business case documentation to store stakeholders
- Presented findings to management with data-backed restocking recommendations
- Demonstrated measurable inventory optimisation opportunity
